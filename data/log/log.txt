21:     *   *Source file:* `data/processed/schools.csv` / `data/processed/schools.geojson`
22: *   **School-Age Demographics (SADD):** Tabular data detailing school-age children population (boys and girls) per LGA.
23:     *   *Source file:* `data/processed/school_age_population.csv`
24: *   **Administrative Boundaries (OCHA):** LGA level polygon shapefiles for Adamawa, Borno, and Yobe states.
25:     *   *Source file:* `data/clean/nga_shp/nga_admin2.shp`
26: 
27: ---
28: 
29: ## 3. Methodology
30: The pipeline processes points, aggregates counts spatially, and calculates a normalized index:
31: 
32: 1.  **Spatial Joins:**
33:     *   Point datasets (Conflict, IDPs, Schools) are projected to WGS 84 (`EPSG:4326`) and joined with LGA boundary polygons to aggregate event counts, total site populations, and school statuses per LGA.
34: 2.  **Indicators & Normalization:**
35:     *   **School Closure Rate:** `closed_schools / (total_schools + 1)` (to prevent division by zero).
36:     *   **Conflict Intensity:** Normalized LGA-level conflict count (0 to 1 scale).
37:     *   **IDP Density:** Normalized LGA-level IDP site population (0 to 1 scale).
38:     *   **School-Age Density:** Normalized LGA-level school-age children population (0 to 1 scale).
39: 3.  **Risk Score Formulation:**
40:     *   A composite index is calculated using weighted indicators:
41:         $$\text{Risk Score} = (\text{Conflict Intensity} \times 0.30) + (\text{IDP Density} \times 0.25) + (\text{School Closure Rate} \times 0.25) + (\text{School-Age Density} \times 0.20)$$
42:     *   The final risk score is normalized between 0 (lowest disruption risk) and 1 (highest disruption risk).
43: 
44: ---
45: 
46: ## 4. Key Insight
47: > [!IMPORTANT]
48: > **Education disruption is highest where large displaced populations and school closures overlap.**
49: > LGAs like **Gwoza**, **Bama**, and **Jere** in Borno State exhibit the highest risk scores because they host both massive IDP camps and have high school closure rates combined with high conflict density. Conversely, high-population urban centers like Maiduguri remain vulnerable due to demographic density, even with fewer school closures.
50: 
51: ---
52: 
53: ## 5. Limitations & Assumptions
54: *   **Data Gaps:** Point coordinates for schools and conflict events are subject to database collection coverage limits and security challenges in reporting. Some remote areas might have underreported events.
55: *   **Normalization Assumptions:** Min-Max normalization assumes a linear increase in risk with density. In reality, risk levels might follow non-linear patterns (e.g., threshold effects where risk increases sharply after a certain capacity is reached).
56: *   **Coordinate Precision:** IDP coordinates were filtered to remove a site recorded outside the BAY states bounding box (located in Taraba State but mislabeled as Yobe).
