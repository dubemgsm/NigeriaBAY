### **Overview of Datasets in Nigeria**
These datasets are commonly used for humanitarian, development, and research purposes in Nigeria to estimate population distribution when census data is older or aggregated at coarse administrative levels[1][2].

*   **GPW (Gridded Population of the World, v4.11):** Produced by CIESIN at Columbia University, this is a "minimally modeled" dataset[1][3]. It distributes census counts (typically from 2006 Local Government Area data in Nigeria) uniformly across grid cells[1][4]. It is useful for global consistency but does not account for the actual spatial distribution of settlements within those areas[1][4].
*   **LandScan:** Developed by Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL), this dataset uses a different approach, distributing national population estimates based on land cover and other geospatial indicators to provide an "ambient" population measure at approximately 1 km resolution (though newer High Definition/HD versions exist at higher resolutions)[5][6][7].
*   **HRSL (High Resolution Settlement Layer):** This dataset provides population estimates at a very high resolution (approximately 30m)[8]. It is often praised for its ability to better delineate settlements—including in urban and rural areas—by identifying the presence of buildings using high-resolution satellite imagery[8][2].

### **Availability and Resources**
*   **Humanitarian Data Exchange (HDX):** You can find various population datasets for Nigeria, including high-resolution density maps and demographic estimates, on the [HDX platform](https://data.humdata.org/)[9][10].

### **Comparison and Accuracy**
Research comparing these datasets in Nigerian contexts (such as in Lagos or Port Harcourt) has noted that:
*   All gridded population datasets may struggle to accurately capture populations in informal urban settlements (slums), often underestimating them[16][17].

Several open-source and open-access population datasets for Nigeria are available for direct download. These datasets are distributed under open licenses—most commonly **Creative Commons Attribution (CC BY 4.0)**—allowing you to use, adapt, and share the data.

Below are the key platforms, what they offer, and the formats available for download:

---

### 1. GRID3 Nigeria Data Portal
GRID3 offers high-resolution, bottom-up population estimates modeled using building footprints and survey data.
*   **What is downloadable:** 
    *   Gridded population estimates (by total and demographic age/sex cohorts).
    *   Settlement extents (delineation of built-up areas).
    *   Operational Ward Boundaries (GIS vectors).
*   **Download Formats:** **GeoTIFF (.tif)** for rasters; **Shapefiles (.shp)** and **GeoPackage (.gpkg)** for vectors; **CSV** for tabular data.
*   **Access:** [GRID3 Nigeria Hub](https://grid3.org/data-hub) or [grid3.gov.ng](https://grid3.gov.ng/)

### 2. WorldPop Open Data Repository
WorldPop provides open-access, high-resolution (100m) gridded population estimates derived from statistical models.
*   **What is downloadable:**
    *   Constrained and unconstrained gridded population counts.
    *   Demographic age/sex structures.
    *   Annual time-series population estimates.
*   **Download Formats:** **GeoTIFF (.tif)**, **ASCII XYZ**, and **CSV**.
*   **Programmatic Access:** Programmatic download is supported via Python APIs and the `wopr` R package.
*   **Access:** [WorldPop Open Population Repository](https://www.worldpop.org)

### 3. Humanitarian Data Exchange (HDX)
HDX is a repository managed by OCHA that compiles datasets from various humanitarian partners working in Nigeria.
*   **What is downloadable:** 
    *   Meta's High Resolution Settlement Layer (HRSL) population density (30m resolution).
    *   GRID3 operational and administrative boundaries.
    *   Displaced populations and survey aggregates.
*   **Download Formats:** **ZIP** archives containing **Shapefiles (.shp)**, **GeoJSON**, **GeoTIFF (.tif)**, and **CSV / Excel (.xlsx)**.
*   **Access:** [HDX Nigeria Datasets](https://data.humdata.org/group/nga)

### 4. UN DESA (World Population Prospects)
For macro-demographic statistics and long-term population projections (national-level).
*   **What is downloadable:**
    *   Historical and projected population numbers (up to 2100).
    *   Fertility, mortality, and migration datasets.
*   **Download Formats:** **Excel (.xlsx)** spreadsheets and flat **CSV** files.
*   **Access:** [UN DESA WPP Download Portal](https://population.un.org/wpp/Download/)
