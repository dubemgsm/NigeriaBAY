Background

Conflict, displacement, and school closures in North-East Nigeria have severely disrupted access to education. These challenges are concentrated in specific local government areas where vulnerable populations are most affected.

About This Analysis

This dashboard identifies priority LGAs where education disruption is most severe by combining conflict data, IDP population, school functionality, and school-age population. It is designed to support rapid, data-driven decision-making. It highlights where immediate interventions should be prioritized.

Key Insight

This indicates that education disruption is not evenly distributed. A small number of local government authorities (LGAs) — primarily in Borno State — account for the highest levels of risk due to overlapping conflict exposure, large displaced populations, and high school closure rates. EBI should prioritize these areas for immediate education interventions.

How Risk is calculated
Risk Score = 30% Conflict + 25% IDP Population + 25% School Closure Rate + 20% School-age Population

This indicates that children in these LGAs are most at risk of losing access to education due to overlapping pressures.

Priority Recommendation

EBI should immediately prioritize education interventions in the following LGAs: Gwoza, Maiduguri, Bama, Jere, Konduga, Damboa, Ngala, Monguno, Dikwa, and Mobbar. Immediate actions should focus on reopening closed schools, supporting displaced children, and restoring access in conflict-affected areas.

Operational Recommendation for Field Teams

EBI should restrict travel and enhance safety protocols during the 7-day windows centered around the high-risk events (Mawlid, Ramadan End, New Year, Easter, and Eid al-Fitr) as conflict intensity significantly increases during these periods. This enables field teams to plan deployments around periods of elevated risk and reduce exposure during high-risk windows.
